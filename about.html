About Us

INTRODUCTION OF DEPARTMENT

As old as Mayo Hospital itself, East Medical Ward also known as Lyallpur Ward, is unit-1 of the department of medicine KEMU. Rich in its legacy of outstanding teaching and training, Model Medical Ward, EMW has been headed by fourteenth professors since partition; of which seven professors were also principals/Vice Chancellors of the King Edward Medical College/University. EMW has been ruling the medical floor for years, and it has become a matter of pride and prejudice for every doctor to work in this department. EMW is currently headed by Prof. Dr. Muhammad Imran (Chairman Department of Medicine). Under his dynamic leadership, EMW is ready to set a new trajectory of excellence and a novel precedent of success. EMW is a fully renovated centrally air-conditioned ward with fully equipped ICU and provision of central oxygen supply to every bed of the ward. The renovated ward was inaugurated by the legends of Medicine (Prof. Akhtar Khan, Prof. Khawaja Sadiq Hussain, and Prof. Mehmood Ali Malik) and its various bays and wings have been named after them in recognition of their priceless contributions to this ward. With the reputation of being the most capacious and well-equipped ward of medicine with dedicated faculty and a vigilant team of trainee doctors, EMW is also heading the Endocrinology, Rheumatology, Gastroenterology, Central Endoscopy Unit, state of the art procedures including ERCP, Liver Clinic Diabetic Clinic, and Diabetic foot care clinic. EMW is also providing a fully functional dialysis unit and bedside Ultrasonography for admitted patients. EMW (having consultants of utmost professional capabilities) is providing evidence-based and up-to-date learning to its undergraduate and postgraduate students through ward rounds, bedside case discussions, ward journal clubs, clinicopathological conferences, Ward Reading Room, Ward Library, and clinical audits. The unit has central role in supervising the research that is conducted in the department of medicine. With a rich past, vibrant present, and ambitious future, EMW is tantamount to an institution per se.
